---
title: Connect Your Calendar
description: Link Google Calendar, Outlook, or Zoom so Rose can book intro calls directly into your schedule.
---

Rose books intro calls directly into your calendar, so connecting it is an essential step. On the **Connect your calendar and email** screen, choose the platform your company uses.

## Your options

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Google Calendar" icon="calendar">
    Rose will book intro calls directly in your Google Calendar.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Outlook Calendar" icon="calendar">
    Rose will schedule meetings and send invites through Outlook.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Zoom" icon="video">
    Rose will create Zoom meetings and attach the links to your bookings.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Connecting

Click **Connect** next to your chosen platform and follow the authorisation prompts. Once it's linked, the option shows a **Connected** badge. You can connect more than one — for example, Outlook for your calendar and Zoom for meeting links.

When at least one calendar is connected, click **Continue**. In a hurry? You can choose **Skip for now** and connect later.

<Callout kind="info">
  You can connect, change, or disconnect calendars any time from the **Integrations** page. At least one calendar must be connected for Rose to book interviews. See [Scheduling Integrations](/integrations/scheduling).
</Callout>

<Callout kind="tip">
  Using a personal Outlook account (such as @outlook.com or @hotmail.com)? Teams links aren't always added to invites automatically — connecting Zoom as well ensures a call link is always attached.
</Callout>
