Rose is the AI recruiter at the heart of Hiros. She sources real candidates, runs your outreach, holds the conversations, and books the calls — so you can focus on interviewing.

Most "AI recruiting" tools are search boxes with a chatbot bolted on. Rose is different. She works the way a world-class talent partner does: she takes a brief, thinks through who would actually be a fit, goes and finds them, reaches out in your voice, and manages every reply until a warm candidate is sitting on your calendar. You stay in control at every step — Rose just does the heavy lifting.

<Callout kind="info">
  You describe who you're looking for. Rose finds them, engages them, and fills your pipeline with warm, interested candidates — without you needing to lift a finger.
</Callout>

## The four jobs Rose does

Rose isn't a single feature — she's an agent that carries a hire from a blank brief all the way to a booked interview. Along the way she plays four distinct roles.

### 1. Rose as a sourcing agent

Rose doesn't just match keywords. You describe who you're looking for in plain language, and she works the problem in steps — clarifying the brief, identifying the kinds of companies your ideal candidate works at today, sizing the talent pool before committing to it, and then retrieving and judging real profiles from a database of **800M+ indexed professionals**.

Rather than dumping a flat list on you, Rose organises her findings into **Candidate Packages** — curated groups of candidates, each with a descriptive label that explains *why* she grouped them that way. She'll often return two or three packages representing different valid interpretations of your brief, so you can see the trade-offs and choose.

<Callout kind="tip">
  Rose recalculates each candidate's **relevant** years of experience — stripping out internships, pre-graduation roles, and time spent in unrelated functions — so the experience number you see reflects work that actually matters for your role, not a raw LinkedIn total.
</Callout>

### 2. Rose as an outreach agent

Once you've selected candidates and launched a sequence, Rose takes over the outreach entirely. She writes and sends a personalised, multi-touch email campaign on your behalf, tailored to each candidate and to the specifics of your role — sent on a sensible business-hours schedule designed to land in inboxes, not spam folders.

### 3. Rose as a conversation agent

When candidates reply, Rose reads every message. She works out whether the reply is genuine, what the candidate actually means — interested, full of questions, not a fit, or asking to opt out — and pulls out any availability they mention. Then she responds in your voice, answers questions, and keeps the conversation moving toward a booked call. Anything she can handle, she handles. Anything that genuinely needs you, she surfaces.

### 4. Rose as a scheduling agent

When a candidate is ready to talk, Rose compares their availability against your connected calendar, finds slots where you're both free, and books the intro call — creating the calendar invite, attaching a video link, and confirming with both sides. Depending on your settings she can book automatically, offer you a shortlist of times to choose from, or simply hand you a ready-to-book candidate.

## How Rose thinks

When Rose evaluates whether a candidate fits, she weighs evidence in a deliberate order — much like an experienced recruiter would:

| Priority | What Rose looks for |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Task similarity** | Has this person actually done the work your role requires — not just held a similar title? |
| **Evidence of output** | Is there concrete proof of results, shipped work, or measurable impact? |
| **Problem exposure** | Have they worked on the kinds of problems, scale, or stage you're hiring for? |
| **Company context** | Does their background line up with your company's industry, stage, and size? |
| **Complexity** | Have they operated at the level of complexity the role demands? |

This is why Rose's results tend to feel hand-picked rather than scraped. She's reasoning about fit, not just filtering rows.

## Built on a multi-model AI architecture

Rose isn't a single model behind a chat window. She runs on a **multi-model architecture** that routes each job to a model purpose-built for it — one for planning and conversation, others for fast candidate judgement, reply analysis, and writing. The result is an agent that can plan, research across many steps, judge profiles, and draft natural replies, all while staying fast and reliable.

You never have to think about any of this. It simply means Rose is good at the specific things recruiting demands: understanding nuance in a brief, reading intent in a reply, and writing email that sounds like a person.

## Your level of control

Rose is built to be trusted, but trust is earned on your terms. For every sequence you choose how much Rose does on her own:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Rose replies automatically" icon="bot">
    End-to-end handling. Rose manages the full conversation, sending replies herself and escalating only the genuinely tricky situations. The fastest, most hands-off mode.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Rose drafts, you approve" icon="pen-line">
    Rose writes every reply, but nothing is sent until you approve it in the Inbox. Full speed of drafting, full human oversight.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="You reply yourself" icon="user">
    Rose stays quiet and you manage the conversation manually. Rose still sources, sends the initial outreach, and tracks the pipeline.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

You can change this setting at any time — even after a sequence has launched.

## The end-to-end workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tell Rose who you're hiring" icon="message-square">
    Describe the role in natural language in the Rose AI search, or build a search manually using filters.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and select candidates" icon="users">
    Browse Rose's candidate packages or your filter results, and select who to engage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up a sequence" icon="list">
    Add job details and customise the outreach emails Rose will send.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch" icon="send">
    Rose reaches out to candidates and manages the conversation from there.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Book interviews" icon="calendar">
    When a candidate is interested, Rose books the call — or hands it to you, ready to confirm.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Built to be trusted

Rose treats messages from candidates as untrusted input and is hardened against attempts to manipulate her through email content, so an unusual reply can never hijack your campaign. And because Rose is AI and can occasionally make mistakes, Hiros keeps you in the loop where it matters: you review candidates before outreach, and you choose how much of the conversation Rose runs on her own.

<Callout kind="warning">
  Rose is powerful, but she's a co-pilot, not a black box. Always review profiles before adding them to a sequence, and pick the reply-automation mode that matches how much oversight you want.
</Callout>
