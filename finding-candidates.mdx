Hiros gives you two ways to find candidates: Rose AI search and Manual Filters. Both draw from the same 800M+ profile database. Rose AI search is the fastest path and where Rose is at her most powerful; Manual Filters give you precise, hands-on control when you want it.

## Rose AI Search

Rose AI search is the fastest way to find candidates. Rather than setting filters manually, you simply describe who you're looking for in natural language — the same way you'd brief a recruiter — and Rose does the research for you.

Under the hood, Rose treats your brief as a problem to work, not a query to run. She'll clarify what's ambiguous, figure out which companies your ideal candidate works at today, size the talent pool before committing, retrieve real profiles, and judge each one against what you care about — then hand you curated packages. It typically takes her a single message to go from brief to a shortlist worth reviewing.

### Starting a Search

From the left sidebar, click the **Rose** icon (the circle logo) to open the Rose home screen.

You'll see the prompt: **"Who are we hiring today?"**

Type a description of the role and candidate you're looking for. You can be as specific or as broad as you like. For example:

> *"I'm looking for an Account Executive with 3–5 years of experience at a NY tech startup"*
>
> *"Find me a senior DevOps engineer based in London with AWS and Kubernetes experience"*

Rose will process your request and return one or more **Candidate Packages**.

### What Rose does behind the scenes

When you send a brief, Rose works it in steps rather than firing off a single search:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Clarifies the brief" icon="message-square">
    If anything is ambiguous, Rose asks a focused question — often with tappable suggestions — or proposes a few candidate archetypes for you to choose between.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Finds the right companies" icon="building-2">
    Rose identifies the companies your ideal hire works at today — competitors, adjacent players, and alumni networks — to use as high-signal starting points.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sizes the pool first" icon="eye">
    Before committing, Rose can preview a search angle — the pool size plus its top companies and titles — so she can refine the approach before retrieving profiles.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Retrieves and judges profiles" icon="user-check">
    Rose pulls real profiles and runs a quick judgement pass against your must-haves, noting evidence and gaps for each candidate.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Understanding Candidate Packages

Each Candidate Package is a curated group of candidates that matches a particular interpretation of your brief. Packages are labelled descriptively (e.g. "AEs at NY tech startups, 3–5 years experience") so you can quickly assess their relevance. Rose often returns two or three packages representing different valid reads of your brief — so you can compare directions instead of second-guessing a single list.

Each package shows:

- The number of candidates ready, and how many are held in reserve
- A preview list of up to 5 candidates, with their current role, company, and key skill tags
- An **Open all** link to view the full package
- An **Add [n]** button to add the entire package to a sequence in one click

<Callout kind="tip">
  **The experience number is smarter than it looks.** Rose recomputes each candidate's *relevant* years of experience — counting only post-graduation, in-function work and excluding internships, student jobs, and unrelated roles. It reflects experience that actually matters for your role, not a raw LinkedIn total.
</Callout>

### Refining results in conversation

Because the search lives in a chat, you can steer Rose the way you would a colleague:

- Ask her to **narrow** by location, seniority, or specific companies.
- Tell her what you **liked and didn't like**, and she'll iterate on the next set.
- Ask her to **filter a package** — cap candidates per company, exclude certain employers, or set a minimum experience bar.
- Ask her to **reveal more** from a package's reserve when you need extra volume.

<Callout kind="warning">
  Rose is AI and can make mistakes. Always review profiles before adding them to a sequence.
</Callout>

### Returning to Previous Searches

Your previous Rose conversations are saved and accessible from the left sidebar under the Rose icon. Click **Chat history** to browse past searches and pick up where you left off.

## Manual Filters

If you prefer to build a search yourself, use the **Search** page (the magnifying glass icon in the left sidebar).

The filter panel on the left lets you narrow your search across six categories. Results update live as you apply filters.

### Available Filters

**Role & Title** — Search by current job title, past job title, job function, and seniority level. You can also exclude specific job titles to remove irrelevant profiles.

**Skills & Keywords** — Filter by skills and keywords, with the option to **Include** or **Exclude** each term. You can also filter by industry (e.g. Software, Tech, Software Development), profile language, and three engagement signals:

- *Recently changed jobs* — candidates who have moved roles recently
- *Posts on LinkedIn* — candidates who are active on LinkedIn
- *In the news* — candidates with recent press mentions

**Location** — Target candidates by city, region, or country.

**Experience** — Filter by total years of experience.

**Company** — Filter by current or past company name, LinkedIn URL, or domain. Targeting specific companies is one of the most powerful ways to identify high-quality candidates — see [Tips & Tricks](/tips-and-tricks) for more.

**Education** — Filter by institution, degree, or field of study.

### Reading Your Results

Once you've applied at least one filter, the results panel will show matching candidate cards. Each card displays:

- Name and profile photo
- Current role and employer
- Location
- Education
- A **View full profile** link to the candidate's LinkedIn profile
- If the candidate is already in one of your sequences, a tag will indicate this

The total match count is shown at the top of the results panel. You can scroll through and select candidates individually using the checkbox on each card, or select all and add them to a sequence.

### Optimising Your Search

Aim for the sweet spot — a pool that's precise enough to be relevant, but not so narrow that you run out of candidates. Start broadly and add filters one at a time, watching how the match count changes.

As a general guide:

- A pool in the hundreds to low thousands is a good starting point
- If your pool is very large (tens of thousands), add more specific filters
- If your pool is very small, consider relaxing one or two criteria

For advanced search techniques including Boolean operators, see [Tips & Tricks](/tips-and-tricks).
